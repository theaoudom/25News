MLBB Hero Tier List 2025: Best Heroes for Ranked and Competitive Play

By Emily White | June 25, 2025 | 16 min read

Mobile Legends: Bang Bang's meta is constantly evolving, with balance updates, new heroes, and shifting strategies influencing which heroes are strongest at any given time. As we move through 2025, understanding the current tier list is crucial for players looking to climb ranked or compete in tournaments.

This comprehensive tier list analyzes heroes across all roles, considering their performance in ranked play, competitive matches, and various skill levels. We'll examine what makes heroes strong, how the meta has shifted, and which heroes you should prioritize learning and playing.

Understanding Tier Lists

Before diving into specific heroes, it's important to understand how tier lists work and their limitations. Tier lists are based on multiple factors including win rates, pick rates, ban rates in competitive play, versatility, and overall impact on matches.

However, tier lists aren't absolute. A hero's effectiveness depends on team composition, player skill, and the current meta. A lower-tier hero in the right hands or the right team can outperform higher-tier heroes. Additionally, tier lists change with balance updates, so staying current is important.

This tier list is organized by role: Tank, Fighter, Assassin, Mage, Marksman, and Support. Within each role, heroes are categorized into S-tier (must-pick/must-ban), A-tier (strong and reliable), B-tier (viable but situational), and C-tier (weak or niche).

Tank Heroes

Tanks are crucial for team composition, providing crowd control, damage absorption, and initiation capabilities. The best tanks can engage fights, protect carries, and control objectives.

S-Tier Tanks

Atlas: Atlas remains one of the strongest tanks due to his game-changing ultimate ability. His ability to pull multiple enemies together can single-handedly win teamfights. His durability and crowd control make him a priority pick in competitive play.

Khufra: Khufra's ability to counter mobile heroes and provide strong crowd control keeps him in S-tier. His bouncing ball mechanic and ultimate make him excellent at both engaging and protecting. He's particularly effective against heroes with dashes and blinks.

Grock: Grock's early game dominance and ability to control the jungle make him valuable. His wall placement can create advantageous situations, and his damage output for a tank is significant. He's a strong roamer who can impact the map early.

A-Tier Tanks

Belerick: Belerick's passive and ultimate make him excellent against physical damage dealers. His ability to reflect damage and provide crowd control makes him a strong counter-pick in certain matchups.

Lolita: Lolita's shield ability makes her valuable against projectile-based heroes. Her ultimate can turn teamfights, though it requires good positioning and timing. She's particularly strong in coordinated team play.

Tigreal: Tigreal remains a reliable tank with strong crowd control. His ultimate's area-of-effect stun can change teamfights, and his overall kit is well-rounded. He's a solid choice for players learning the tank role.

Fighter Heroes

Fighters provide a balance of damage and durability, often serving as front-line damage dealers or off-tanks. The best fighters can sustain through fights while dealing significant damage.

S-Tier Fighters

Yu Zhong: Yu Zhong's ability to deal damage while building tank items makes him incredibly strong. His ultimate's transformation and area damage, combined with his sustain, make him a top-tier fighter who can carry games.

Paquito: Paquito's mobility, damage, and versatility keep him in S-tier. His ability to build damage or tank items depending on team needs, combined with his skill-based gameplay, makes him valuable in skilled hands.

Benedetta: Benedetta's mobility and burst damage make her a strong fighter/assassin hybrid. Her ability to dash through enemies and escape makes her difficult to pin down, and her damage output is significant.

A-Tier Fighters

Chou: Chou's versatility and crowd control keep him relevant. His ability to build tank or damage, combined with his mobility and ultimate's displacement, make him valuable in various team compositions.

Thamuz: Thamuz's sustain and area damage make him strong in extended fights. His ability to control space and deal consistent damage makes him valuable, especially in the exp lane.

Terizla: Terizla's crowd control and damage make him a strong teamfight fighter. His ultimate's area stun and his ability to build tank items while dealing damage make him valuable.

Assassin Heroes

Assassins excel at eliminating high-priority targets and controlling the jungle. The best assassins can snowball games through early kills and objective control.

S-Tier Assassins

Ling: Ling's mobility and damage output make him one of the strongest assassins. His ability to traverse walls and burst down targets makes him difficult to counter, especially in skilled hands.

Hayabusa: Hayabusa's ability to split push and assassinate targets keeps him relevant. His shadow clones provide escape and damage, making him versatile and difficult to pin down.

Gusion: Gusion's burst damage and mobility make him a top-tier assassin. His combo potential can eliminate squishy targets quickly, and his skill ceiling rewards skilled players.

A-Tier Assassins

Lancelot: Lancelot's mobility and damage make him strong, though he requires skill to maximize. His ability to dash through enemies and deal damage makes him effective in teamfights and skirmishes.

Natalia: Natalia's stealth and burst damage make her valuable for picking off targets. Her ability to control vision and eliminate key enemies makes her strong in coordinated play.

Saber: Saber's targeted ultimate makes him effective against mobile heroes. His ability to lock down and eliminate specific targets makes him valuable, especially as a counter-pick.

Mage Heroes

Mages provide magic damage and often crowd control. The best mages can control teamfights, deal area damage, and provide utility to their teams.

S-Tier Mages

Lunox: Lunox's dual form mechanics and burst damage keep her in S-tier. Her ability to switch between light and dark forms provides versatility, and her damage output is significant.

Yve: Yve's ultimate ability to control large areas makes her incredibly strong. Her ability to zone enemies and deal consistent damage makes her valuable in teamfights and objective control.

Valentina: Valentina's ability to copy enemy ultimates makes her versatile and strong. Her kit provides damage, mobility, and the unique ability to counter-pick by copying powerful enemy ultimates.

A-Tier Mages

Pharsa: Pharsa's long-range damage and mobility make her strong. Her ability to deal damage from safety and her ultimate's area damage make her valuable in teamfights.

Cecillion: Cecillion's scaling damage makes him strong in longer games. His ability to stack damage and his ultimate's execute make him valuable, especially as games progress.

Lylia: Lylia's area damage and crowd control make her strong. Her ability to control space and deal consistent damage makes her valuable in teamfights.

Marksman Heroes

Marksmen provide sustained physical damage and are crucial for taking objectives and dealing damage in teamfights. The best marksmen can carry games with proper positioning and farm.

S-Tier Marksmen

Beatrix: Beatrix's multiple weapon forms provide versatility and damage. Her ability to adapt to different situations and her damage output make her one of the strongest marksmen.

Claude: Claude's mobility and area damage make him strong. His ultimate's area damage and his ability to reposition make him valuable in teamfights.

Melissa: Melissa's ability to deal damage from range and her ultimate's crowd control make her strong. Her kit provides both damage and utility, making her valuable.

A-Tier Marksmen

Wanwan: Wanwan's mobility and damage make her strong, though she requires skill to maximize. Her ability to dash and deal damage makes her difficult to pin down.

Brody: Brody's ability to deal damage while building tank items makes him unique. His scaling damage and durability make him valuable, especially in extended fights.

Layla: While often considered a beginner hero, Layla's range and damage scaling make her viable in certain situations. Her ability to deal damage from extreme range can be valuable with proper positioning.

Support Heroes

Supports provide utility, healing, and crowd control. The best supports can enable their teams and control teamfights through their utility abilities.

S-Tier Supports

Mathilda: Mathilda's mobility and utility make her one of the strongest supports. Her ability to engage, protect, and provide movement speed makes her valuable in various team compositions.

Rafaela: Rafaela's healing and crowd control make her strong. Her ability to sustain her team and provide utility makes her valuable, especially in extended fights.

Angela: Angela's ultimate ability to attach to allies and provide support makes her unique. Her ability to enable carries and provide utility makes her valuable.

A-Tier Supports

Estes: Estes' healing makes him valuable in sustain-focused compositions. His ability to keep his team healthy makes him strong, especially in longer games.

Diggie: Diggie's ability to counter crowd control makes him valuable as a counter-pick. His ultimate's area immunity to crowd control can turn teamfights.

Carmilla: Carmilla's ability to link with enemies and share damage makes her unique. Her crowd control and utility make her valuable in certain team compositions.

Meta Trends and Considerations

Understanding current meta trends helps contextualize tier lists. As of 2025, several trends are shaping the meta:

Early Game Dominance: Heroes that can impact the game early, especially in the jungle and during first objectives, are highly valued. This favors aggressive junglers and strong early-game tanks.

Versatility: Heroes that can fill multiple roles or adapt to different situations are increasingly valuable. This includes heroes that can build both damage and tank items effectively.

Teamfight Control: Heroes with strong teamfight abilities, especially area crowd control and damage, remain important. The ability to control teamfights often determines match outcomes.

Mobility: Mobile heroes that can engage and disengage effectively are valuable. The ability to reposition and escape makes heroes harder to counter.

How to Use This Tier List

While tier lists provide guidance, success in MLBB requires more than just picking high-tier heroes. Consider these factors:

Team Composition: A lower-tier hero that fits your team composition may be better than a higher-tier hero that doesn't. Consider how heroes work together.

Player Skill: A hero you're skilled with may outperform a higher-tier hero you're unfamiliar with. Mastery matters more than tier placement for individual players.

Counter-Picking: Sometimes, picking a hero specifically to counter an enemy pick is more valuable than picking a high-tier hero. Understanding matchups is crucial.

Meta Adaptation: The meta changes with balance updates. Stay current with patch notes and adjust your hero pool accordingly.

Conclusion

This tier list provides a snapshot of the MLBB meta in 2025, but remember that tier lists are guides, not rules. Success in Mobile Legends comes from understanding heroes, team composition, and game strategy, not just picking high-tier heroes.

Focus on mastering a few heroes across different roles rather than trying to play every S-tier hero. Depth of knowledge and skill with specific heroes often trumps tier placement. Additionally, understanding why heroes are in certain tiers helps you make better decisions about picks and bans.

As the meta continues to evolve, stay updated with balance changes and competitive play. The heroes that are strong today may shift with the next update, so flexibility and continuous learning are key to long-term success in Mobile Legends: Bang Bang.
